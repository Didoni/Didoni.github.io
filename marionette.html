Marionette - device for simulating 3D shapes

The Marionette system was one of the contributions of my postgraduate research degree in the University of Bristol.

It worked by supplying individual tilt feedback to four fingers – index, middle, ring and little.

Operating Marionette is similar to the use of a mouse on a desktop – users can place a hand on top of the device and drive it over a horizontal exploration plane. Meanwhile, the four plates tilt to copy the surface orientation at the accessed point on the virtual object. Consequently, the user feels like sweeping her fingers over the shape.

Shapes simulated by inducing orientation information alone

  • Concave and convex
  • Simulate quadratic curved shapes
  • Saddle shapes
  • Simple and Complex Shapes
  • Static and dynamic
  • Maps, pie charts and bar charts
  • Live depth images from a Kinect sensor

Design

Two servos were used to produce a 2DOF of rotation for each finger place- tilt along one axis (pitching) and tilt along the other axis (rolling). The range of angles the U-joint can achieve is [-45, 45].
